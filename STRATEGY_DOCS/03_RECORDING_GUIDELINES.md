# ScoutVerse: Player Recording Guidelines for AI Accuracy

## The Three Golden Rules for High-Accuracy Analysis
To achieve the best results, players must follow specific recording techniques. A steady camera is vital because the physics brain works by subtracting the background; a tripod or steady prop ensures the AI doesn't think the pitch itself is moving. An elevated view from the stands is elite because it provides a clear view of the pitch geometry, making speed and distance calculations much more accurate. Finally, players should not zoom too much so the AI can see the space around them to judge tactical awareness.

## Highlights versus Targeted Drills
Players should be encouraged to upload two types of content. Game Highlights are used for evaluating tactical awareness and decision-making. Specific Drills, like a 30-meter sprint or a 5-meter shuttle run recorded on a tripod, provide a "clean" environment. In these drills, the technical skills rating becomes incredibly precise.

## The ScoutVerse Certified Badge
We should implement a "High-Confidence Data" badge for players. Videos recorded following these guidelines get this badge on their profile. This tells scouts that the metrics, such as a 32 KM/H top speed, are verified and mathematically accurate.

## App Integration
A "Recording Guide" component should be added to the upload screen. Using simple icons for tripods, elevated angles, and short clips will show investors that we prioritize data integrity and professional standards.
